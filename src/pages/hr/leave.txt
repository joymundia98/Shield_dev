Here's a comprehensive list of common leave types that organizations typically offer. Some might vary depending on location, company policy, or industry.

### **1. Paid Time Off (PTO)**

* **Vacation Leave**: Time off for rest, travel, or personal reasons.
* **Personal Leave**: Paid time off for personal matters that are not necessarily vacation.
* **Sick Leave**: Time off for illness or medical treatment.

### **2. Unpaid Leave**

* **Unpaid Personal Leave**: Time off for personal reasons without pay.
* **Unpaid Family Leave**: Time off to care for a family member or personal health issue, but without compensation.

### **3. Family and Medical Leave**

* **FMLA Leave (Family and Medical Leave Act)**: In the U.S., eligible employees can take up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave for certain family or medical reasons, such as childbirth, adoption, or caring for a seriously ill family member.
* **Maternity/Paternity Leave**: Leave for the birth of a child or to care for a newborn.
* **Adoption Leave**: Leave to take care of a newly adopted child.
* **Parental Leave**: Broad category encompassing maternity, paternity, and adoption leave.

### **4. Bereavement Leave**

* Leave for grieving the death of a loved one, usually paid for a certain number of days.

### **5. Public Holiday Leave**

* Paid leave for national, state, or religious holidays.

### **6. Jury Duty Leave**

* Paid or unpaid leave for fulfilling jury duty responsibilities.

### **7. Military Leave**

* Leave for military service or training.

### **8. Study or Educational Leave**

* Time off to attend classes, workshops, or study for academic purposes, sometimes with pay.

### **9. Compassionate Leave**

* Paid or unpaid leave to attend to urgent family situations, such as the serious illness or death of a close family member.

### **10. Religious Leave**

* Leave for religious observances or holidays.

### **11. Leave for Voting**

* Leave granted to employees to participate in elections or voting.

### **12. Sabbatical Leave**

* Extended leave, often unpaid, offered for personal growth, study, or rest after a certain period of employment.

### **13. Compassionate Care Leave**

* Leave for caregivers of individuals with life-threatening illnesses, sometimes as part of broader family medical leave policies.

### **14. Sick Leave (for care of family members)**

* Time off to care for a sick family member or dependent.

### **15. Adoption/Foster Care Leave**

* Time off to settle in or take care of a new foster child or adopted child.

### **16. Extended Leave**

* Leave for extended time off due to illness, recovery from surgery, or other serious health conditions.

### **17. Time Off for Organ Donation**

* Leave granted to employees who are donating an organ.

### **18. Leave for Rehabilitation**

* Leave for individuals who need time off for addiction treatment or rehabilitation.

---

Some companies also offer more flexible or specific leave policies, especially in countries with strong worker rights or progressive company cultures.
